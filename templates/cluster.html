Segmentation Analysis (K-Means)

What is K-Means Clustering?

K-Means is an unsupervised machine learning algorithm that groups similar data points into clusters. Its main objective is to minimize the distance between data points and the center of their assigned group (the centroid).

How it works

  • Initialization: "K" random points are chosen as initial centroids.
  • Assignment: Each person is linked to the nearest centroid.
  • Update: Centroids move to the average position of all assigned points.
  • Convergence: This repeats until the groups are stable and no longer change.

Understanding your Clusters

In this analysis, the population was divided into 3 groups based on Age, Income, and Bills:

  • Cluster 0: Young profiles with entry-level income/expenses.
  • Cluster 1: High-income and high-spending senior profiles.
  • Cluster 2: Mid-range income or transitional profiles.
